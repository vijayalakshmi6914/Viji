Left Arm

The left arm plays a crucial role in manipulation, grasping, and fine motor skills. It consists of:

  • Humerus: Upper arm bone connected to the shoulder and elbow joints.
  • Radius and Ulna: Forearm bones that allow for rotation and movement of the wrist and hand.
  • Elbow Joint: Enables bending and straightening of the arm, supported by ligaments and muscles.
  • Biceps and Triceps: Muscles responsible for arm movement, including lifting and extending.
  • Hand and Fingers: Includes bones, joints, and muscles for dexterity and grip strength.
